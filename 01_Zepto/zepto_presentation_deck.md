## SLIDE 1: TITLE SLIDE
*   **Title:** Zepto: Accelerating the Densification Flywheel
*   **Subtitle:** Strategic Roadmap to EBITDA Positivity Post-IPO
*   **Visual:** Minimalist dark background with Zepto brand purple accent line. Company logo bottom right.
*   **Slide Objective:** Set a premium, serious tone for the board/investors.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "Good morning. Today we are not discussing speed—we are discussing structural profitability. We will review how Zepto transitions from a hyper-growth logistics player to a high-margin retail media network."

## SLIDE 2: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
*   **Title (Action):** Zepto commands ~30% of the $14B Quick Commerce market, but must pivot from aggressive acquisition to margin expansion to justify its $7B valuation.
*   **Content:** 3-column layout. 
    1) **Current State:** Decacorn trajectory, ₹22.6K Cr GMV, brutal cash burn. 
    2) **The Moat:** Dark store density & predictive AI (not just 10-min speed). 
    3) **Strategic Imperative:** Scale Zepto Ads, optimize unit economics, defend against Flipkart.
*   **Icons:** 1) Rocket (Scale), 2) Castle (Moat), 3) Target (Imperative).
*   **Speaker Notes:** "This is the entire presentation on one page. We have won the speed war. Now we must win the margin war. Our core thesis is that Zepto Ads and density are the only paths to sustainable EBITDA."

## SLIDE 3: MACRO ENVIRONMENT
*   **Title (Action):** Q-Commerce is Cannibalizing Modern Trade, driven by time-poor urban consumers and premiumization.
*   **Visual/Charts:** Stacked bar chart showing shift of grocery spend from Unorganized (Kirana) -> Modern Trade (Supermarkets) -> E-Commerce -> Q-Commerce (2020 to 2030 projections).
*   **Animation:** Wipe left to right on the bar chart to show the growth of the Q-Commerce segment.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "Quick commerce isn't just taking share from Amazon; it's replacing the weekend supermarket trip. The $1 Trillion grocery TAM is wide open."

## SLIDE 4: COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
*   **Title (Action):** The market has consolidated into a brutal oligopoly; Zepto has aggressively captured the #2 position.
*   **Visual/Charts:** Mekko Chart or Market Share Pie Chart. Blinkit (45%), Zepto (30%), Instamart (23%).
*   **Icons:** Logos of competitors.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "We are operating in a highly consolidated market. While Blinkit leads due to Zomato's top-of-funnel advantage, Zepto's standalone focus has allowed us to leapfrog Swiggy Instamart."

## SLIDE 5: THE FINANCIAL REALITY
*   **Title (Action):** Revenue has scaled exponentially to ₹22.6K Cr, but massive dark-store Capex continues to drive deep net losses.
*   **Visual/Charts:** Dual-axis line and bar chart. Bars = Revenue (growing). Line = Net Margin (improving but still deeply negative).
*   **Slide Objective:** Acknowledge the cash burn transparently.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "The top-line growth is historic. However, our ₹5,900 Cr loss reflects the reality of building physical infrastructure in 80+ cities. The question investors have is: when does the line cross zero?"

## SLIDE 6: DECONSTRUCTING THE MOAT (VRIO)
*   **Title (Action):** Zepto’s 10-minute speed is commoditized; the true sustainable advantage is our proprietary predictive routing.
*   **Visual:** VRIO Framework Table (Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, Organized) with checkmarks. Highlight "AI Routing" and "Dark Store Density" in green.
*   **Slide Objective:** Shift the narrative away from generic "fast delivery."
*   **Speaker Notes:** "If Flipkart wants to deliver in 10 minutes, they can buy the real estate. What they cannot buy is our 4 years of hyper-local predictive data and our bipartite matching algorithms."

## SLIDE 7: UNIT ECONOMICS BREAKDOWN
*   **Title (Action):** Achieving store-level profitability requires pushing Average Order Value (AOV) above ₹600.
*   **Visual/Charts:** Waterfall chart showing a single order. Starting at AOV (₹450), subtracting COGS, Logistics (₹45), Dark Store Ops (₹30), leaving a negative contribution margin. Then showing how an AOV of ₹600 turns it positive.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "This waterfall chart is our battlefield. At ₹450 AOV, we lose money on every delivery. By increasing cart sizes, the fixed cost of logistics amortizes, pushing us into the black."

## SLIDE 8: THE PROFIT ENGINE (ZEPTO ADS)
*   **Title (Action):** Zepto Ads is scaling at 33x with 80% margins, transitioning Zepto from a logistics layer to a Retail Media Network.
*   **Visual/Charts:** Exponential growth curve showing Ad Revenue jumping to ₹1,636 Cr.
*   **Slide Objective:** Introduce the Amazon-style media playbook.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "This is the most important slide in the deck. We are building a high-margin media business on top of a low-margin logistics business. Brands are paying us for closed-loop attribution at the point of purchase."

## SLIDE 9: CATEGORY EXPANSION (ZEPTO CAFE)
*   **Title (Action):** Zepto Café captures the high-margin QSR impulse-buy segment, driving immediate AOV uplift.
*   **Visual:** Process flow diagram showing existing dark store + espresso machine = instant café. No new real estate required.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "By utilizing our existing dark store footprint and riders, Zepto Café allows us to compete with Zomato food delivery with near-zero marginal infrastructure costs."

## SLIDE 10: PRIVATE LABELS
*   **Title (Action):** Expanding in-house brands (Relish, Daily Good) recaptures the 15-20% FMCG manufacturer margin.
*   **Visual/Charts:** Comparison bar chart: Margin on 3rd Party Rice (10%) vs. Margin on Daily Good Rice (35%).
*   **Speaker Notes:** "We control the digital shelf. By pushing our own private labels in staples and fresh produce, we capture the entire value chain."

## SLIDE 11: ZEPTO PASS
*   **Title (Action):** Subscription models lock in customer loyalty, reducing CAC and driving order frequency.
*   **Visual:** Flywheel graphic: Subscribe -> Free Delivery -> Buy More Often -> Lower CAC -> Better Margins.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "Zepto Pass isn't about giving away delivery fees. It's about changing consumer behavior so they use us 10 times a month instead of 3, drastically lowering our blended acquisition cost."

## SLIDE 12: THE "DENSIFICATION" STRATEGY
*   **Title (Action):** Increasing nodes per city (avg 21) reduces delivery radii, increasing rider throughput per hour.
*   **Visual/Charts:** Heatmap of Mumbai showing overlapping 1.5km dark store radii.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "We aren't expanding to 500 new cities. We are saturating the top 15. Density means a rider does 4 deliveries an hour instead of 2. That halves our logistics cost."

## SLIDE 13: KEY RISKS (PESTLE)
*   **Title (Action):** Gig-worker reclassification and deep-pocketed new entrants pose the highest existential threats.
*   **Visual:** 2x2 Risk Matrix (Impact vs. Probability). "Labor Laws" and "Flipkart Price War" in the top right quadrant (High/High).
*   **Speaker Notes:** "We cannot ignore the regulatory environment. If Karnataka's Gig Worker bill becomes national, our variable labor costs will crystallize into fixed payroll. We must prepare preemptive welfare structures."

## SLIDE 14: THE IPO CATALYST
*   **Title (Action):** The ₹11,000+ Cr IPO provides the balance sheet required to outlast competitors in the capital expenditure war.
*   **Visual:** Pie chart showing "Use of Proceeds" (Dark store expansion, tech infra, EV fleet).
*   **Speaker Notes:** "The IPO is not a cash-out; founders are retaining equity. This is a war chest to build the physical infrastructure that Amazon and Flipkart will struggle to replicate quickly."

## SLIDE 15: THE 90-DAY PLAN
*   **Title (Action):** Immediate focus post-IPO: Secure prime urban real estate and accelerate brand onboarding for Zepto Ads.
*   **Content:** 30-60-90 day Gantt chart.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "In the next 90 days, we execute on two fronts: locking in long-term leases in Tier 1 bottlenecks, and expanding the Zepto Atom analytics dashboard for our FMCG partners."

## SLIDE 16: THE 1-YEAR ROADMAP
*   **Title (Action):** Year 1 targets achieving store-level EBITDA positivity across 80% of mature dark stores.
*   **Content:** 3 pillars: Ad Revenue = 10% of GMV. AOV = ₹650. Rider retention up 20%.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "By the end of FY27, we need the street to see a clear path to corporate profitability. That means pushing our mature cohorts strictly into the black."

## SLIDE 17: THE 5-YEAR VISION
*   **Title (Action):** Zepto will evolve into the default urban operating system for instant commerce, transcending groceries.
*   **Visual:** Expanding concentric circles: Groceries -> Café -> Pharmacy -> Fashion -> Electronics.
*   **Speaker Notes:** "In five years, 10-minute delivery won't just be for tomatoes. It will be for iPhones, Zara shirts, and prescription meds. Zepto will be the infrastructure layer of Indian retail."
