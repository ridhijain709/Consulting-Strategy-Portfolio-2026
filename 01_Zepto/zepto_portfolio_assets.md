## 2. STAR INTERVIEW STORY
*(For behavioral interviews when asked: "Tell me about a time you analyzed a complex problem" or "Show me your strategic thinking")*

**Situation:** 
As I prepare for my MBA and target top-tier consulting roles, I wanted to build proof-of-work that goes beyond academic case studies. I chose to analyze the most brutal, cash-burning sector in India right now: Quick Commerce, specifically focusing on Zepto as they prepare for an ₹11,000 Cr IPO.

**Task:** 
The challenge was cutting through the PR hype. Everyone talks about "10-minute delivery," but I needed to understand the actual unit economics. How does a company burn ₹5,900 Cr a year but maintain a $7B valuation? I set out to build a McKinsey-grade Master Research Document based entirely on public data.

**Action:** 
I started by analyzing their UDRHP filings and tracking their dark store expansion. I built a waterfall chart of their unit economics, proving that at an AOV of ₹450, they lose money on every delivery due to fixed logistics costs. I then mapped their revenue streams and discovered that the real valuation driver wasn't groceries—it was Zepto Ads, which grew 33x in two years to ₹1,636 Cr at 80% margins. I synthesized this into a VRIO analysis, showing that speed is a feature, but predictive AI routing and hyper-local density are their true moats.

**Result:** 
I translated this research into a 30-page strategy document and a 20-slide board presentation. It completely changed my perspective on retail strategy—I now understand that Q-commerce players are actually building high-margin retail media networks subsidized by a low-margin logistics layer. This project has become the cornerstone of my consulting portfolio.

## 5. CASE COMPETITION SUMMARY (Framework)
*(If you use this Zepto research for a B-School Case Comp)*

**The Prompt:** *How can a loss-making Quick Commerce company achieve profitability in India?*

**The Strategy (The 3-Pillar Approach):**
1.  **Monetize the Digital Shelf (Retail Media):** Treat the app like prime real estate. Scale Zepto Ads to FMCG brands demanding closed-loop attribution. (Target: Ads = 10% of GMV).
2.  **Amortize the Fixed Logistics Cost (AOV Expansion):** Push Average Order Value from ₹450 to ₹650 via Zepto Café (high-margin QSR impulse buys) and premium electronics.
3.  **Densification over Expansion (Operational Leverage):** Halt Tier-3 city expansion. Focus on maximizing throughput in the top 15 metros to reduce delivery radii to <1.5km, doubling rider efficiency per hour.
